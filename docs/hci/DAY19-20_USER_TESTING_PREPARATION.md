## 🔬 Research Methodology Standards

### Think-Aloud Protocol (Concurrent)

**Rationale**: Participants verbalize thoughts while performing tasks
**Benefits**:
- Reveals cognitive processes
- Identifies confusion points in real-time
- Uncovers mental models
- Expects feature expectations

**Best Practices**:
1. **Instruction phrasing**: "Please think out loud as you complete this task. Tell me what you're looking for, what you're thinking, and what you expect to happen."
2. **Prompting when silent**: "What are you thinking now?" or "What are you looking for?"
3. **Neutral demeanor**: Don't react to mistakes or successes
4. **Non-leading questions**: Avoid "Can you see the X button?" → Instead: "What would you like to do next?"

### Semi-Structured Interview (Post-Test)

**Rationale**: Open-ended questions with flexibility to probe
**Benefits**:
- Rich qualitative data
- Unexpected insights
- Participant-driven priorities
- Context for quantitative scores

**Best Practices**:
1. **Ask "Why" repeatedly**: "Why did you do that?" "Why do you say that?"
2. **Probe for specifics**: "Can you give me an example?" "Tell me more about..."
3. **Silence is powerful**: Pause after answers to encourage elaboration
4. **Confirm understanding**: "So what I'm hearing is... Is that right?"

- Control for order effects (randomize task order if needed)

## 🚨 Risk Mitigation Strategies

### Risk #1: No-Show Participants

**Probability**: Medium (20-30% no-show rate typical)
**Impact**: High (affects statistical power)
**Mitigation**:
- Recruit 6-7 participants for 5 slots
- Send reminder 24 hours before
- Send reminder 1 hour before
- Have backup participant list

### Risk #2: Technical Issues During Test

**Probability**: Low (Day 18 integration successful)
**Impact**: Critical (blocks data collection)
**Mitigation**:
- Test environment dry-run on Day 20
- Backup device ready
- Hotspot/alternative internet ready
- Screen recording backup method

### Risk #3: Participant Confusion/Frustration

**Probability**: Medium (new feature)
**Impact**: Medium (affects SUS/NPS)
**Mitigation**:
- Extended introduction if needed
- Provide hints after 30 seconds of struggle
- Document confusion points (valuable data!)
- Reassure participant: "Remember, we're testing the system, not you."

### Risk #4: Poor SUS Score (<70)

**Probability**: Low (98.6/100 HCI score historically)
**Impact**: Medium (reputational, but valuable data)
**Mitigation**:
- Accept honest feedback (don't lead participants)
- Use data to identify improvements
- Compare to industry benchmarks
- Plan Sprint 5 improvements if needed
