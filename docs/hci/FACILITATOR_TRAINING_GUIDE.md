# Facilitator Training Guide

**Purpose**: Comprehensive training for user testing facilitators
**For**: Paper Detective Sprint 4 User Testing (Day 21-22)
**Facilitators**: Team members conducting user sessions
**Participants**: 5 users

---

## 🎯 Facilitator Role Overview

### Your Responsibilities

**As a facilitator, you are:**
- ✅ **Guide**: Lead participants through tasks
- ✅ **Observer**: Watch and record behavior
- ✅ **Neutral Party**: Don't influence or judge
- ✅ **Data Collector**: Capture quantitative and qualitative data
- ✅ **Professional**: Represent the research team

**As a facilitator, you are NOT:**
- ❌ **Teacher**: Don't instruct or train
- ❌ **Defender**: Don't explain or justify design decisions
- ❌ **Influencer**: Don't lead participants to solutions
- ❌ **Tester**: Participants test the system, not you
- ❌ **Participant**: Your opinion doesn't matter, theirs does

## 🎤 Core Technique: Think-Aloud Protocol

### What is Think-Aloud?

**Definition**: Participants verbalize their thoughts, feelings, and actions while completing tasks.

**Benefits**:
- Reveals mental models and expectations
- Identifies confusion points in real-time
- Uncovers decision-making processes
- Provides context for behaviors

### How to Administer Think-Aloud

**Initial Instruction** (say this verbatim):
> "As you complete each task, please think out loud. Tell me what you're looking for, what you're thinking, and what you expect to happen. There are no wrong answers - we're interested in your thought process."

**When Participant is Silent** (prompt every 30 seconds):
- "What are you thinking now?"
- "What are you looking for?"
- "What do you expect to happen when you click that?"
- "What's going through your mind right now?"

**When Participant Asks "Is this right?"**:
- ⚠️ **DON'T** say "Yes" or "No"
- ✅ **DO** say "Whatever you think is right"
- ✅ **DO** say "There's no right or wrong way"
- ✅ **DO** say "What would you normally do here?"

## 🚫 Facilitator Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

### #1: The Helpful Helper ❌

**What it looks like**:
- Participant: "I can't find the button"
- Facilitator: "It's right there, click the blue button"

**Why it's bad**:
- Inflates success rate (not real data)
- Hides usability problems
- Skews task completion times

**What to do instead**:
- "What would you expect to find here?"
- "What are you looking for?"
- Wait 30 seconds, then offer hint as last resort

---

### #2: The Defensive Designer ❌

**What it looks like**:
- Participant: "This is confusing"
- Facilitator: "Actually, it's designed that way because..."

**Why it's bad**:
- Makes participant feel stupid
- Stops honest feedback
- Wastes time explaining instead of listening

**What to do instead**:
- "Tell me more about what's confusing"
- "What would make it less confusing?"
- Write down their complaint (it's valuable data!)

---

### #3: The Leading Questioner ❌

**What it looks like**:
- Facilitator: "Can you see the Generate Brief button?"
- Participant: "Oh, yes, I see it now" (just to please facilitator)

**Why it's bad**:
- Participants want to be helpful
- They'll say yes even if they don't see it
- Skews data toward "success"

**What to do instead**:
- "What would you like to do next?"
- "What are you looking for?"
- "Where would you expect that to be?"

---

### #4: The Silent Observer ❌

**What it looks like**:
- Participant struggles in silence for 5 minutes
- Facilitator says nothing, takes no notes

**Why it's bad**:
- Participant gets frustrated
- May abandon task (data loss)
- Misses opportunity for deeper insights

**What to do instead**:
- Prompt every 30 seconds: "What are you thinking?"
- Take notes continuously
- After 30 seconds of struggle, offer help

## 🎯 Handling Common Situations

### Situation #1: Participant Can't Find Something

**Scenario**: Participant is looking for a button/link for 60+ seconds

**What to do**:
1. **0-30 seconds**: Say nothing, just observe
2. **30-60 seconds**: "What are you looking for?"
3. **60+ seconds**: "Would you like me to point you in the right direction?"

**If they say yes**:
- "The button you're looking for is [general location]"
- Let them find the exact button themselves

**Record**:
- Time to find
- Where they looked first
- What they expected to see

---

### Situation #2: Participant Makes an Error

**Scenario**: Participant clicks the wrong thing or makes a mistake

**What to do**:
- **DO NOT** correct them immediately
- **DO NOT** say "That's wrong"
- **DO** observe if they notice the error themselves
- **DO** note the error in your log

**If they notice and correct themselves**:
- Great data! Shows they can recover

**If they don't notice and continue**:
- Let them proceed (unless it blocks the task)
- Note the error as a finding

---

### Situation #3: Participant Asks Technical Questions

**Scenario**: "How does the AI work?" or "What database are you using?"

**What to say**:
- "That's a great question, but we're focused on your experience using the tool today. We can discuss technical details after the test if you're interested."

**Why**:
- Technical explanations bias their experience
- Focus remains on UX, not implementation
- Stay on schedule

---

### Situation #4: Participant Gets Frustrated or Angry

**Scenario**: Participant sighs, gets agitated, says "This is stupid"

**What to do**:
1. **Acknowledge**: "I can see you're frustrated"
2. **Validate**: "It's okay to feel frustrated"
3. **Reassure**: "Remember, you're helping us find problems. That's valuable."
4. **Continue**: "Would you like to take a break or continue?"

**If they want to stop**:
- Let them stop (ethical obligation)
- Still collect SUS and NPS (partial data is valuable)
- Ask interview questions about their frustration

**Record**:
- What triggered frustration
- Task they were on
- Severity (mild/moderate/severe)

---

### Situation #5: Participant is Too Quiet

**Scenario**: Participant completes tasks without saying anything

**What to do**:
- **More frequent prompting**: Every 20-30 seconds
- **Explicit reminders**: "Remember to think out loud"
- **Encouragement**: "Your thoughts are really helpful"
- **After the task**: "Walk me through what you just did"

**If they still won't talk**:
- Accept it (some people are naturally quiet)
- Rely on observed behavior
- Ask more questions during interview phase

---

### Situation #6: Participant Finishes Tasks Very Quickly

**Scenario**: Participant completes all 6 tasks in 10 minutes (should be 20-30)

**What to do**:
1. **Verify**: Did they actually complete the tasks? Or skip ahead?
2. **Probe**: "Tell me about what you just did"
3. **Extend**: "Can you show me how you would use this in your real research?"
4. **Explore**: "Is there anything you'd like to explore further?"

**Record**:
- Actual completion times
- Quality of completion (did they actually do it?)
- Comments about ease of use

## 📝 Note-Taking Best Practices

### What to Record

**ALWAYS Record**:
- ✅ Task completion times (start/stop)
- ✅ Success/failure status for each task
- ✅ Verbal quotes (verbatim, or close to it)
- ✅ Error/confusion points
- ✅ Frustration indicators (sighs, furrowed brow, negative comments)

**WHEN POSSIBLE Record**:
- 📍 Navigation path (key pages visited)
- 📍 Click sequence (if video recording, can review later)
- 📍 Facial expressions (if indicative of emotion)
- 📍 Hesitations/pauses

**DON'T BOTHER Recording**:
- ❌ Every single click (too detailed)
- ❌ Obvious successful interactions (boring)
- ❌ Your own opinions or reactions

### Note-Taking Short-Hand

**Use abbreviations**:
- PT = Participant
- ↓ = Scrolled down
- ↑ = Scrolled up
- 🖱️ = Clicked
- ⌨️ = Typed
- ⏸️ = Paused/hesitated
- ❓ = Confused/uncertain
- 😊 = Positive reaction (smiled, said "cool", etc.)
- 😠 = Frustrated (sighed, negative comment)
- 🏁 = Task completed

**Example note**:
```
Task 2: Generate Brief
PT hovered over header, ↓ to find button
🖱️ clicked "Generate Brief"
⏸️ waited 5s, ⏸️ looked at screen
😊 "Oh, that was fast!"
🏁 completed in 45s
Quote: "I like how it shows progress"
```

## 🧠 Bias Prevention

### Common Facilitator Biases

**Confirmation Bias**:
- Seeking evidence that confirms your beliefs
- **Example**: Noticing problems you expected, missing surprises
- **Prevention**: Stay curious, expect the unexpected

**Social Desirability Bias**:
- Participants want to please you
- **Example**: Saying "I love it!" when they don't
- **Prevention**: Neutral tone, don't show excitement

**Hawthorne Effect**:
- Participants behave differently because they're observed
- **Example**: Being more careful than normal
- **Prevention**: Tell them "act naturally", minimize your presence

**Experimenter Effect**:
- Your expectations influence participant behavior
- **Example**: Leaning forward when they're close to right answer
- **Prevention**: Maintain neutral body language, sit back

### Bias Prevention Checklist

**Before the session**:
- [ ] Review tasks (but don't memorize "right" paths)
- [ ] Set expectations: "No right or wrong answers"
- [ ] Prepare neutral tone and body language

**During the session**:
- [ ] Keep facial expressions neutral
- [ ] Don't nod or shake head
- [ ] Sit back, don't lean forward
- [ ] Don't react to mistakes or successes
- [ ] Use "Tell me more" instead of leading questions

**After the session**:
- [ ] Review notes for bias
- [ ] Flag assumptions you made
- [ ] Separate observations from interpretations

## ⏱️ Timing and Pacing

### Session Schedule (30-45 minutes total)

**Part 1: Introduction** (5 minutes)
- Welcome and consent
- Pre-test questionnaire

**Part 2: Tasks** (20 minutes)
- Task 1: Upload PDF (5 min)
- Task 2: Generate Brief (3 min)
- Task 3: Search (4 min)
- Task 4: Export (2 min)
- Task 5: Organize (3 min)
- Task 6: Navigate (3 min)

**Part 3: Post-Test** (5 minutes)
- SUS questionnaire (2 min)
- NPS question (1 min)
- Feature ratings (2 min)

**Part 4: Interview** (10 minutes)
- Semi-structured questions

**Part 5: Debrief** (5 minutes)
- Thank participant
- Answer their questions
- Compensation (if applicable)

### Keeping on Schedule

**If running ahead of time**:
- Extend interview with more questions
- Ask about their research workflow
- Probe deeper into interesting topics
- Let them explore the system freely

**If running behind**:
- Skip feature ratings (less critical)
- Shorten interview (pick top 3 questions)
- Offer to continue via email (for follow-up)
- Don't rush SUS or NPS (critical data)

**Never skip**:
- SUS (10 questions) - critical
- NPS with follow-up - critical
- Task completion status - critical

## 🎭 Professional Demeanor

### Appearance and Behavior

**Dress**: Professional but approachable (business casual)

**Tone**: Friendly, neutral, interested

**Body Language**:
- Sit slightly back (don't crowd)
- Maintain neutral facial expression
- Nod occasionally (but don't signal right/wrong)
- Take notes visibly (shows you're listening)

**Voice**:
- Speak clearly and at moderate pace
- Use neutral tone
- Don't react to mistakes or successes
- Sound interested but not excited

### Handling Stress

**If something goes wrong** (technical bug, crash):
1. **Stay calm**: Don't show panic
2. **Acknowledge**: "It looks like there's a technical issue"
3. **Reassure**: "This is not your fault, it's a system issue"
4. **Document**: Note exactly what happened
5. **Continue**: Move to next task or questionnaire

**If participant challenges you**:
- **Don't argue** (ever)
- **Don't defend** the design
- **Do listen** to their perspective
- **Do document** their complaint
- **Do validate**: "That's a helpful perspective"

## 📊 Data Quality Checklist

### During the Session

**For each task, ensure you record**:
- [ ] Start time
- [ ] End time
- [ ] Completion status (Success/Failed/Abandoned)
- [ ] Error count
- [ ] Key quotes (at least 1 per task)
- [ ] Frustration level

**At the end of the session**:
- [ ] SUS questionnaire completed (all 10 questions)
- [ ] NPS score and follow-up response recorded
- [ ] Interview questions answered (at least top 5)
- [ ] Overall participant mood noted

### After the Session (Within 1 hour)

**Immediate actions**:
- [ ] Review notes while fresh
- [ ] Fill in any missing details
- [ ] Calculate SUS score
- [ ] Flag critical issues (any that blocked participants)
- [ ] Save screen/audio recordings

**Before next session**:
- [ ] Reset test environment
- [ ] Print fresh tracking sheet
- [ ] Check equipment (recording, timer)
- [ ] Take a break (facilitating is exhausting!)

## 🚨 Emergency Protocols

### If Participant Distressed

**Signs**: Very upset, angry, or stressed

**Actions**:
1. **STOP** the session immediately
2. **ACKNOWLEDGE**: "I can see you're very upset"
3. **OFFER**: "Would you like to stop?"
4. **COMPENSATE**: Still provide compensation (ethical)
5. **DOCUMENT**: What happened (without judgment)

### If Technical Failure

**Signs**: App crashes, can't proceed

**Actions**:
1. **APOLOGIZE**: "I'm sorry, we're having technical difficulties"
2. **OFFER**: Reschedule or stop now
3. **COMPENSATE**: Still provide compensation
4. **DOCUMENT**: Technical failure details
5. **REPORT**: To team immediately

### If Participant Inappropriate

**Signs**: Harassment, offensive language, unsafe behavior

**Actions**:
1. **END** session immediately
2. **LEAVE** if unsafe
3. **REPORT** to team/PI immediately
4. **DOCUMENT** incident
5. **NO** compensation if terminated due to behavior

## 📚 Quick Reference Card

**Key Phrases**:
- "Think out loud as you work"
- "What are you looking for?"
- "What did you expect to happen?"
- "There's no right or wrong answer"
- "Tell me more about that"

- "Click the X button" (too directive)

- Wait 30 seconds before helping
- Prompt every 30 seconds if silent
- Keep session to 45 minutes max

- ALWAYS: Times, status, quotes
- PRIORITY: SUS, NPS, task completion
- OPTIONAL: Navigation paths, facial expressions
